The ball of the end

The project Everywhere at the end of time de The Caretaker is a musical enigma. This journey to the complete loss of memory makes us lose our memory and our bearings.

Time flies by. Sometimes the past pursues us, the present escapes us and the future makes us anxious. Leyland Kirby takes us through a labyrinth of time with his album Everywhere at The End of Time. under the name The Caretaker. This British composer has created a timeless work that spans six parts released between 2016 and 2019. Thus, the project represents 6 and a half hours of listening in total and mixes sound experiments, collages and noise music.

Paintings by Ivan Seal illustrating each episode of Everywhere at the End of Time

The nostalgia of the 1930s

The nostalgia of the 30s both in content and form, this album addresses the themes of time and the deterioration of memory until the complete destruction of oneself. Inspired by the haunted ball scene from The Shining by Stanley Kubrick , Leyland Kirby will bring the memories of ghosts of the past to life one last time. They notably uses soundtracks from the 1930s and 1940s that they will rework.

Ball scene from The Shining

Between memory and forgetfulness

All that follows is true is one of the songs that starts the album. It’s one of the most powerful compositions. Indeed, it marks the transition from the first to the second stage towards forgetfulness and madness. The artist has mixed the sweetness of a nostalgic music, that of Maurice Winnick ( It Looks Like Rain In Cherry Blossom Lane -1932) and static noises, repetitions, ruptures. This mixture creates a feeling of unease while keeping the calm and reassuring aspect of nostalgia.

Music disc of It looks like rain in cherry blossom lane

Leyland Kirby gives birth to music that is both familiar and unknown. He takes us to the border between memory and forgetfulness.
