The time to do nothing

Telework is a new organization of work. It was born from the health situation caused by the pandemic, but also from the aspirations of our generation for more freedom and flexibility thanks to digital tools.

For nearly two years, the pandemic has put the world on hold. New methods of communication, training and work had to be learned. Even if mentalities still have a hard time accepting it, teleworking is now part of the way we work. Our generation has embraced and adapted to this new opportunity.

Better professional and personal management

Personally, I think it is an ideal organization on several levels. Telework allows you to manage your time, whether professional or personal, at your own pace. You can spend your evening finishing your work and take some time during the day to relax. Also, this organization is more personal friendly. It saves time: commuting, sleep, non-work activities… Studies have shown that productivity was increased. It is actually easier to concentrate without the noise and distractions that interrupt us at the workplace It is also a faster, simpler communication space with colleagues who may be geographically distant. These exchanges are then not only professional but also allow to discover other cultures.

Mutual trust for tomorrow

My hope is that telework offers a relationship of mutual trust between employee and employer. I hope that technology will develop to create tools or collaborative spaces that are even more user-friendly, more efficient and that preserve human relationships. For example, we could imagine all meeting in virtual reality, playing or discussing in the context of work…
